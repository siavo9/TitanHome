Month-by-Month Execution Plan

MONTH 1 — Foundation (April)
Target: $5,000

Marketing Setup (Week 1-2)

  • Set up Google Business Profile — CRITICAL. Add photos of work, service areas, hours. This is your #1 lead source.
  • Get on Yelp, Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, and Nextdoor — these platforms send leads immediately
  • Launch Facebook Business page. Post before/after photos of EVERY job
  • Start Google Ads — budget $15-20/day on "junk removal near me", "cleanout services [city]", "trash removal [city]"
  • Print 500 door hangers/flyers — hit neighborhoods near recent jobs
  • Order vehicle magnets or wraps with phone number + website

Relationship Building (Week 2-4)

  • Visit 10 real estate offices. Drop off cards. Realtors need cleanout help for listings CONSTANTLY
  • Contact 10 property management companies — eviction cleanouts are steady, recurring work
  • Join your local Facebook community groups. Offer a "first job 10% off" deal
  • Introduce yourself to 5 general contractors — offer to be their debris removal partner

Operations

  • Create a simple CRM (even a Google Sheet) — track every lead, quote, and job
  • Set up QuickBooks or Wave for invoicing — look professional from Day 1
  • Build a pricing guide so you can quote fast on calls
  • Get liability insurance if you don't have it — required for commercial work
MONTH 2 — Momentum (May)
Target: $10,000

Lead Generation Ramp

  • Increase Google Ads to $25/day if Month 1 ROI was positive
  • Ask EVERY completed customer for a Google review — goal: 15+ reviews by end of May
  • Post 3x per week on social media — before/after photos, time-lapse videos of cleanouts
  • Start a referral program: $50 cash or credit for every referral that books
  • Knock on doors in neighborhoods where you just completed a visible job

Revenue Boosters

  • Start quoting home repairs alongside every cleanout — target 30% upsell rate
  • Reach out to 5 commercial property managers for office/retail cleanouts
  • Partner with a local moving company — they always have clients who need junk hauled
  • List on HomeAdvisor/Angi — these generate warm leads willing to pay
MONTH 3 — Growth Mode (June)
Target: $15,000

Scaling Operations

  • Hire or bring on a second crew if you're turning down work
  • Raise prices 10-15% — you now have reviews and reputation to justify it
  • Offer "summer cleanout specials" — garages, basements, yards
  • Build relationships with estate attorneys — they handle estate sales and need cleanout crews
  • Create a "Titan Home Maintenance Package" — bundle cleanout + basic repairs for landlords

Marketing Optimization

  • Review which ad keywords and platforms are converting best — double down on winners
  • Start collecting video testimonials from happy customers
  • Launch a simple email newsletter to past customers — "seasonal reminders" to book again
MONTH 4 — Acceleration (July)
Target: $20,000
  • Target commercial accounts aggressively — one commercial contract can be worth $2K-5K/month
  • Run "Beat the Heat" summer promotions — yard cleanups, garage cleanouts
  • Add a "same-day service" premium option (+25% surcharge)
  • Expand service area by 15 miles if your core area is saturated
  • Aim for 30+ Google reviews — this puts you above most competitors
  • Start tracking customer lifetime value — repeat customers are your goldmine
MONTH 5 — Scale (August)
Target: $23,000
  • You should have 2-3 recurring commercial accounts by now
  • Launch "back to school" cleanout campaigns for families
  • Consider adding dumpster rental as a service line — high margin, low labor
  • Hire a part-time admin/dispatcher if call volume is overwhelming you
  • Negotiate better rates at dump/recycling facilities based on volume
  • Start building SOPs so your team can run jobs without you present
MONTH 6 — Close Strong (September)
Target: $27,000
  • Push hard on estate cleanouts — fall is peak season for estate sales
  • Re-engage every past customer with a "fall prep" offer
  • Lock in Q4 commercial contracts before year-end cleanups start
  • Evaluate what got you here — cut what didn't work, triple down on what did
  • Begin planning for $200K Year 2

Your Daily CEO Routine

This is non-negotiable. Do this every single day.

6:30 AM — Morning Prep (30 min)

Check messages, emails, and missed calls. Respond to every lead within 15 minutes of waking up. Speed wins jobs.

7:00 AM — Daily Outreach (30 min)

Contact 3 new potential partners: realtors, property managers, contractors, or commercial properties. Every. Single. Day. This is how you build a pipeline.

7:30 AM – 5:00 PM — Execute Jobs

Run your scheduled jobs. Take before/after photos of EVERY job. Get a Google review before you leave the site.

5:00 PM — End-of-Day Admin (30 min)

Send all invoices same day. Follow up on open quotes. Update your CRM. Post one photo/video to social media.

8:00 PM — Plan Tomorrow (15 min)

Confirm next day's jobs. Prep materials list. Set outreach targets for morning.
